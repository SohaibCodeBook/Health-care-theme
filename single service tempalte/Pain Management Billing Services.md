Section 7: FAQs
Eyebrow: FAQS
H2: Pain management billing questions we hear most
Q1: Why should pain management practices outsource their billing?
 Outsourcing pain management billing gives your practice specialists in procedure coding, prior authorizations, and payer audit documentation. Because interventional procedures carry high reimbursement values, a single billing error costs far more than in other specialties. Outsourcing protects that revenue while freeing your staff.
Q2: Do you handle prior authorizations for procedures?
 Yes. We manage prior authorizations for epidurals, nerve blocks, radiofrequency ablations, and other interventional procedures before they happen. Getting authorization right up front prevents the most expensive category of pain management denials and keeps your procedure schedule moving.
Q3: How do you prevent denials on injection claims?
 We scrub every claim against payer-specific coding rules, bundling edits, and frequency limits before submission. Fluoroscopy guidance, bilateral procedures, and multi-level injections each carry unique billing rules, and our team applies them correctly the first time so claims pay without appeals.
Q4: Can you support us through payer audits?
 Yes. Pain management practices face more payer audits than most specialties. Perform Practice Solutions maintains clean documentation trails on every claim and helps you respond to records requests, so audits become a routine process instead of a revenue threat.
Q5: Which EMR systems do you work with?
 We work inside the systems your practice already uses, including Tebra, AthenaHealth, CureMD, and other common EMRs. There is no software migration required to start, so switching to Perform Practice Solutions does not disrupt your workflows.
Q6: How transparent is your billing process?
 Completely transparent. You see where every dollar stands, including claims, A/R, denials, and patient balances. We deliver monthly report reviews and quarterly deep analysis, and your team gets direct chat access to our billers.
